Custom Vectorworks Plug-In Tools

I coded two new tools for Vectorworks called "Select By Something" and "Direct Info". With the first, you can choose any field, click a fixture, and all fixtures that have the same value in that field as the one clicked will be highlighted. You can also manually specify a field value to select all fixtures with that value. With the second, you can click a fixture to see a more concise menu than the object info palette while also having a gel color preview.

A study was done on the effectiveness of these tools as a project for the Media Arts and Technology class "Designing Expressive Technologies".

Data Visualization

The UCSB Global Media Technologies and Cultures lab does a lot of work in collecting data from various company reports around they world. What they were looking for was a way to make that information digestable to the general public. I developed various interactive data visualizations in javascript that gave both a clear and intuitive sense of the information conveyed. It was also important that these visualizations were generative, adapting dynamically to whatever data is input, making these pieces reusable.

This example demonstrates the land ownership of companies under different categories. The goal here was to make the data, areas in the millions of square feet, more understandable by comparing it to well known sizes.

Projects in Allolib

UCSB is home to the Allosphere, a 30-foot tall sphere with full 360 projection coverage inside of it. I'm in a graduate level class in the Media Arts and Technology program where I am learning to create visuals for the Allosphere through its c++ framework, allolib. It's an exciting mix of object oriented programming, computer graphics, and physics simulation.
